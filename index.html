AIR CONDITIONER :A GUIDE TO KEEPING YOUR AIR CONDITIONER RUNNING SMOOTHLY ALL SUMMER


Summertime is a great time to relax and enjoy the weather, but it can also be a time when your air conditioner needs some extra attention. Here are seven tips on how to keep your AC running smoothly all summer long.

Keep the area around your AC unit clear

One of the most important things you can do to keep your air conditioner running smoothly is to keep the area around the unit clear of debris and obstructions. This includes things like leaves, dirt, and even lawn furniture. All of these items can block airflow and make it harder for your AC to do its job. In addition, be sure to keep the area around your AC unit free of any flammable materials.

Another important thing you can do to keep your air conditioner running smoothly is to have it serviced by a professional regularly. This will ensure that the unit is clean and free of any potential problems. Servicing also helps to extend the life of your AC unit.

Finally, make sure that you are using the proper size air conditioner for the space you have available. An air conditioner that is too small will not be able to cool your home effectively, while an air conditioner that is too large will use more energy than necessary, resulting in higher utility bills.

Change your air filter regularly


Another key to keeping your air conditioner running smoothly is to change the filter regularly. Most experts recommend changing it at least once a month, but if you live in an area with a lot of pollen or dust, you may need to change it more often. A dirty filter will make your AC work harder than it needs to and can even lead to a breakdown.

The condenser and evaporator coils are what remove the heat from your home, so they must be able to do their job efficiently. You can clean them yourself with a brush and some cleaner explicitly made for coils, or you can hire a professional to do it for you.

Keep the coils clean


The coils in your AC unit are responsible for transferring heat. If they become dirty, it will make it harder for your AC to do its job and can even lead to a breakdown. To clean the coils, simply remove them and rinse them with a garden hose. You can also use a coil cleaning solution, but be sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions.

Get routine maintenance


One of the best ways to keep your air conditioner running smoothly is to get routine maintenance done by a professional. This should be done at least once a year, but twice a year is even better. During maintenance, the technician will clean the coils, change the filter, and inspect the unit for any potential problems.

If you live in an area with a lot of pollen or other airborne allergens, you may need to change your filter more often. You can check the condition of your filter by holding it up to a light. If you can see through it, it’s time for a new one.

When you first turn on your air conditioner for the season, be sure to give it a few hours to cool down your home before turning it off again. This will help prolong its lifespan.

Upgrade your thermostat


If you have an older thermostat, it might be time to upgrade to a newer model. Newer thermostats are more accurate and can help your AC run more efficiently. You can save money on your energy bill and have greater control over the temperature in your home by upgrading to a newer thermostat.

There are many different types of thermostats available on the market, so it’s important to do your research to find the one that will work best for your home and your needs. Some factors you might want to consider when choosing a new thermostat include:

  • Ease of use: You want a model that is easy to operate and program.
  • Accuracy: Look for a model that is highly accurate so you can trust the temperature readings.
  • Energy efficiency: Choose a model that can help your AC run more efficiently, saving you money on your energy bill.
  • Compatibility: Make sure the thermostat you choose is compatible with your AC unit.

Another simple way to keep your home cool is to close the blinds or curtains during the day. This will help block out the sun’s heat and make it easier for your AC to keep the temperature down.
